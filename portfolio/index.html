Visual portfolio for the graduate handbook assistant. I tried to keep the tone plain and a little awkward on purpose, because that is closer to how I actually talk about the project.

Open the assistant

Problem

I lose things in PDFs. Not because I am careless, but because a handbook is long and my questions do not arrive as neat keywords. They arrive as “wait, what was the rule about…” at 11pm. For this class we were supposed to think about impact beyond the usual ways people hear about research, and I kept coming back to something boring but real: the document already exists. Finding the right paragraph is the hard part.

So I built a chat front end on top of the EESD PhD Graduate Student Handbook. You ask in normal language. The app pulls chunks from the indexed PDF and tries to answer from that text. When it is not in there, it is supposed to say so instead of bluffing.

Voice (the feature, and why it showed up)

Voice was not in my first sketch. I was thinking screen first. When I talked to people in class about the idea, a few of them kept coming back to audio: eyes tired, multitasking, or just not wanting to read another wall of policy on a laptop. That stuck. I wired speech to text and text to speech into the same little app so it stayed one demo, not a side project I pretend counts.

The part that made me feel dumb (then fixed)

First version sent almost everything through handbook search. Someone would type “how are you” as a joke or a habit, and the assistant would still go hunting in the PDF and end up saying it could not find that in the handbook. Technically correct. Socially weird. I did not like how that felt when I watched other people try it.

I added routing so each message gets sorted into chitchat versus handbook lookup, using the recent conversation so short follow ups still make sense. Small talk goes to a normal conversation prompt. Handbook questions still go through retrieval, a rewrite step for messy follow ups, relevance scoring, and citations when the match is actually good. If the match is bad, the app backs off instead of forcing a page number.

What is actually in the repo

Nothing fancy, just pieces that had to work together:

  • PDF chunked and stored in Chroma so search is over the handbook, not over my memory.
  • An LLM step that rewrites the latest question using a short window of chat history so “what about exceptions” is not orphaned.
  • A similarity score threshold so weak hits do not get treated like gospel.
  • Separate prompts for handbook answers versus casual replies so the tone matches what the user is doing.
  • Voice endpoints in the same FastAPI app as the page you are reading.

Try it yourself

Showcase

I did the gallery walk with our class. I am not naturally great at pitching. I practiced saying three things in plain order: what the tool is, who I built it for, and why voice plus grounded answers matter for stress and trust. People asked good questions. One was about hallucinated answers. I showed how citations show up when retrieval is strong, and what happens when the score is low. Another was about updating the handbook. I said the honest version: this is a class prototype tied to one PDF file, and anything “real” would need someone in the program to own updates. I would rather admit that than oversell it.

What I would change next time

I would start explanations even slower for strangers. I would budget more time for retrieval tuning earlier instead of noticing awkward answers late. If this ever left the classroom, I would want a real conversation with the program about maintenance and accessibility testing, not just my assumptions.

Note for grading (EGR 535 rubric)

Audience and reach: EESD PhD students navigating the handbook, plus voice after class feedback. Creativity and risk: routing split and API work I had not done before, plus being strict about weak retrieval. Prototype communication: simple UI, grounded answers, citations when retrieval wins. Showcase: photos above, plus the pitch and two questions described in the showcase section. Reflection: the routing mistake, honesty about limits, and what I would change next time.
